Additional Information about Maha Camp

Mahama Camp Background

o Mahama camp began in 2015 and currently hosts 69,000 refugees from Burundi. The camp is the largest and youngest of the six refugee camps within Rwanda. MINEMA and UNHRC are responsible for directing the refugee response in Rwanda– including Mahama camp. The two entities provide essential resources by coordinating and leading other UN agencies (e.g., UNICEF) and project partners (e.g., NGOs, local communities).

o Mahama Camp has the greatest number of interventions to improve the infrastructure and shelter of refugees. 6,907 duplex semi-permanent shelters have been constructed in Mahama by 2019; however, resources are overstretched, and the camp is overcrowded (UNHRC, 2020b) 

Mahama Demographic

o The majority of refugees in Mahama are from one of two regions – the province of Kirundo in northern Burundi or the province of Bujumbura Mairie near the capital (Sandra, 2017). *Note: regional conflict exists between the two regions and are often based on ‘generalized ascription of statements around who is a real refugee’, economic disparities, favoritism, and responsibilities of their current situation (Sandra, 2017 p.8).
